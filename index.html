SoundMateü¶ªüì¢


Soundmate is an innovative IoT-based smart wearable designed to cater to the needs of the hearing impaired. This cutting-edge device leverages the power of Edge Computing to provide real-time information about the surrounding environment to its users through a combination of Audio-Tactile Conversion (ATC) and Audio-Visual Conversion (AVC). The wearable features a microphone that captures sounds from the user's surroundings, ranging from everyday sounds like birds chirping and crowd chatter to potentially critical noises such as sirens and car horns. These audio inputs are then processed by a sophisticated Deep Learning model, enabling the device to identify and classify specific sounds. What sets Soundmate apart is its proactive approach to user safety and awareness. It actively alerts the wearer when it detects sounds that could potentially pose harm or demand their attention, such as ambulance sirens or car horns. Moreover, Soundmate offers a seamless user experience by extending its functionality to a companion mobile application. This means that in addition to receiving alerts through vibrations or tactile feedback, users can also access visual notifications on their smartphones, further enhancing their situational awareness. The users can also set custom words for which they wish to receive alerts.

KisaanLinküçÉ


KisaanLink is an end to end solution for farmers to sell their produce and buyers to buy the same with no middlemen in the process. The solution achieves Cost effectiveness and helps to deliver most fresh vegetables and fruits to the buyer. There is also the most optimal use of transportation. The transportation of the orders to the buyer is based on the concept of truck pooling which involves dynamic allotment of produce of farmers to trucks based on existing loads alloted for a truck and the destination of the truck. Also, more transparency is achieved by allowing the farmers and the buyers to track the orders live in trucks with their number plates and live location. The buyers with more frequent orders are incentivized and also farmers which sell their produce on a daily basis are given cost cuts in transport commission.
